What is generative artificial intelligence(AI)?

Generative AI is essentially a type of artificial intelligence that is trained on things such books, research papers, videos, and other information, which results in the AI being able to generate text, video, or sound.

Why is it concerning?

Many people are concerned about the usage of generative AI; particularly, creatives such as writers, graphic designers, and others working in similar fields share the concern that generative AI may take their jobs. In Minnesota, there are roughly 50,000 people working in the art and entertainment industry, and many of their jobs are affected by the prevalence of generative AI(Current Employment Statistics (CES) 2025). The largest example of these concerns boiling over is the 2023 Writers Guild of America (WGA) strike, where writers struck for months over these very concerns.

Eventually, the WGA and the Alliance of Motion Picture and Television Producers, whom the WGA were striking against, were able to come to an agreement that outlined the following rules:

  • “Neither traditional AI (technologies including those used in CGI and VFX) nor generative AI (GAI, meaning artificial intelligence that produces content including written material) is a writer, so no written material produced by traditional AI or GAI can be considered literary material.”
  • “If a company gives a writer written material produced by GAI that hasn’t been previously published or exploited, that material doesn’t count as assigned material (for compensation purposes) or source material (for determining credits), and can’t be used to disqualify a writer for separated rights. In other words, a company can’t give you an AI-generated screenplay and pay you a rewrite fee—you would be treated as the first writer of the screenplay.”
  • “A writer can choose to use AI when performing writing services if the company consents and the writer follows any applicable company policies. But the company can’t require the writer to use AI software (e.g., ChatGPT) when performing writing services.”
  • “The Company must disclose to the writer if any materials given to the writer have been generated by AI or incorporate AI-generated material. The WGA reserves the right to assert that exploitation of writers’ material to train AI is prohibited by MBA or other law.”

(Artificial Intelligence 2025)

While agreements like this one are a step forward, the laws in the United States that deal with the use of generative AI are still lacking.
